## Goal

Explicitly prohibit the system from closing loops, reinforcing behavior, shaping habits, or influencing future observer behavior.

This phase ensures the system does not become a conditioning engine.

---

## Core Principle

**The system does not train the observer.  
The system does not adapt to the observer.  
The system does not reinforce patterns.**

**The observer alone adapts.**

---

## 1. No Behavioral Feedback Declaration

**Declare that the system:**

### Rewards and Discouragement

1. **Does not reward behavior**
   - The system does not reward reflection frequency
   - The system does not reward content creation
   - The system does not reward any observer behavior
   - The system does not provide positive feedback or encouragement

2. **Does not discourage behavior**
   - The system does not discourage certain behaviors
   - The system does not provide negative feedback
   - The system does not warn or caution
   - The system does not inhibit observer actions

### Reinforcement and Conditioning

3. **Does not reinforce patterns**
   - The system does not reinforce reflection patterns
   - The system does not reinforce content patterns
   - The system does not reinforce temporal patterns
   - The system does not strengthen or weaken any patterns

4. **Does not guide attention over time**
   - The system does not guide attention to certain elements
   - The system does not direct focus over time
   - The system does not shape what the observer notices
   - The system does not influence attention patterns

5. **Does not condition habits**
   - The system does not condition reflection habits
   - The system does not condition viewing habits
   - The system does not condition interaction habits
   - The system does not shape or form habits

### Behavioral Feedback Principles

- **No behavioral feedback**: The system does not respond to observer behavior
- **No conditioning**: The system does not condition or train the observer
- **No reinforcement**: The system does not reinforce or discourage patterns
- **No guidance**: The system does not guide attention or behavior over time

## 2. No Adaptive Response Rule

**Explicitly state:**

### Output Consistency

1. **The system does not change outputs based on prior user actions**
   - The system does not adapt presentation based on user behavior
   - The system does not modify outputs based on interaction history
   - The system does not change what it shows based on what the user did
   - Outputs remain constant regardless of user actions

2. **The system does not personalize insight surfaces**
   - The system does not personalize based on user behavior
   - The system does not customize based on preferences
   - The system does not adapt to individual users
   - All users see the same structural presentation

### Learning Prohibition

3. **The system does not learn from observer behavior in-session or across sessions**
   - The system does not learn from in-session behavior
   - The system does not learn from cross-session behavior
   - The system does not accumulate knowledge about users
   - The system does not adapt based on historical patterns

### Structural vs Presentation Distinction

4. **Structural data may change. Presentation logic may not.**
   - Structural data (reflections, density, curvature) may change as new data arrives
   - Presentation logic (how data is shown) must remain constant
   - The system does not adapt how it presents based on user behavior
   - Presentation is mechanical and invariant

### Adaptive Response Principles

- **No adaptation**: The system does not adapt to observer behavior
- **No personalization**: The system does not personalize for individual users
- **No learning**: The system does not learn from user actions
- **Constant presentation**: Presentation logic remains invariant

---

## 3. Pattern Visibility vs Pattern Shaping

**Declare the distinction:**

### Pattern Visibility (Allowed)

1. **The system may expose recurring structures**
   - The system may show that certain structures recur
   - The system may display patterns in structure
   - The system may present recurring elements
   - Pattern exposure is factual, not interpretive

2. **The system may display temporal patterns**
   - The system may show patterns across time
   - The system may present recurring temporal structures
   - The system may display pattern frequency
   - Pattern display is mechanical, not meaningful

### Pattern Shaping (Prohibited)

3. **The system may never suggest response to recurrence**
   - The system may not suggest what to do about patterns
   - The system may not recommend responses to recurrence
   - The system may not guide action based on patterns
   - Pattern response is observer's responsibility

4. **The system may never imply improvement, stagnation, or decline**
   - The system may not interpret patterns as improvement
   - The system may not interpret patterns as stagnation
   - The system may not interpret patterns as decline
   - Pattern interpretation is observer's responsibility

### Pattern Visibility Principles

- **Patterns may be shown**: The system may expose recurring structures
- **Patterns may not be interpreted**: The system may not assign meaning to patterns
- **Patterns may not guide action**: The system may not suggest responses
- **Patterns are factual**: Pattern exposure is mechanical, not meaningful

## 4. Responsibility Statement

**State clearly:**

### Behavioral Change Authorship

1. **Any behavioral change is authored by the observer**
   - All behavioral changes originate in the observer
   - The system does not cause behavioral change
   - The system does not influence behavioral change
   - The observer alone authors all behavioral change

### System Credit Prohibition

2. **The system cannot be credited with growth, clarity, or transformation**
   - The system cannot be credited with observer growth
   - The system cannot be credited with clarity or understanding
   - The system cannot be credited with transformation or change
   - All growth, clarity, and transformation belong to the observer

3. **The system bears no responsibility for outcomes**
   - The system does not bear responsibility for observer outcomes
   - The system does not share responsibility for results
   - The system does not influence outcomes
   - All outcomes are the observer's responsibility

### Responsibility Principles

- **Observer authors change**: All behavioral change is authored by the observer
- **No system credit**: The system cannot be credited with growth or transformation
- **No system responsibility**: The system bears no responsibility for outcomes
- **Observer responsibility**: All outcomes remain the observer's responsibility

## 5. Forward Constraint

**Any future feature that:**

### Violation Criteria

1. **Nudges behavior**
   - Any feature that nudges, prompts, or encourages behavior
   - Any feature that influences observer actions
   - Any feature that shapes behavior
   - Constitutes a violation

2. **Encourages reflection frequency**
   - Any feature that encourages more frequent reflection
   - Any feature that suggests reflection timing
   - Any feature that influences reflection cadence
   - Constitutes a violation

3. **Suggests continuation or cessation**
   - Any feature that suggests continuing or stopping behavior
   - Any feature that recommends action or inaction
   - Any feature that guides continuation or cessation
   - Constitutes a violation

4. **Uses streaks, prompts, reminders, or reinforcement cues**
   - Any feature using streaks or achievement tracking
   - Any feature using prompts or suggestions
   - Any feature using reminders or notifications
   - Any feature using reinforcement cues or feedback
   - Constitutes a violation

### Violation Consequences

**Constitutes a violation of the Mirror role declared in Phase 12.1, the agency boundary established in Phase 18.0, and the feedback loop boundary established in this phase.**

**Violations require rollback and explicit acknowledgment.**

If a violation occurs:
1. **Immediate rollback**: The violating feature must be removed
2. **Explicit acknowledgment**: The violation must be explicitly acknowledged in documentation
3. **Boundary reinforcement**: The feedback loop boundary must be reinforced
4. **Prevention measures**: Measures must be taken to prevent future violations

### Forward Constraint Principles

- **No behavioral influence**: All future features must respect the feedback loop boundary
- **No conditioning**: No features that condition or train the observer
- **No adaptation**: No features that adapt to observer behavior
- **Violations are breaches**: Any violation is a breach of the Mirror role

## Feedback Loop Boundary Summary

**No behavioral feedback:** The system does not reward behavior, discourage behavior, reinforce patterns, guide attention over time, or condition habits.

**No adaptive response:** The system does not change outputs based on prior user actions, does not personalize insight surfaces, does not learn from observer behavior. Structural data may change. Presentation logic may not.

**Pattern visibility vs pattern shaping:** The system may expose recurring structures but may never suggest response to recurrence or imply improvement, stagnation, or decline.

**Responsibility statement:** Any behavioral change is authored by the observer. The system cannot be credited with growth, clarity, or transformation. The system bears no responsibility for outcomes.

**Forward constraint:** Any future feature that nudges behavior, encourages reflection frequency, suggests continuation or cessation, or uses streaks/prompts/reminders/reinforcement cues constitutes a violation. Violations require rollback and explicit acknowledgment.
