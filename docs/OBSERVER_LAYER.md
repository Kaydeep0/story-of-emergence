This document defines the **Observer layer** as an architectural invariant, not a feature specification.

It describes **stance, constraints, and success criteria**, not UI components or implementation details.

---

## What the Observer Layer Is

The Observer layer is **continuity of self over time**.

It is a witnessing stance that:
- Remembers what mattered before
- Notices drift and change
- Names recurring tensions
- Holds themes without forcing resolution
- Provides accompaniment, not coaching

It is the layer that answers: **What does this mean in the arc of my life?**

---

## What It Is Not

The Observer layer is **not**:
- A lens (it is not Weekly, Yearly, or Lifetime)
- A page (it is not a route or view)
- A dashboard (it is not metrics-first)
- Output (it is not a report or summary)
- Metrics-first (it does not lead with numbers)

It is a **stance**, not a feature.

---

## Why the System Feels Unfinished Without It

Without the Observer layer, there is a **relational gap**.

The system can tell you:
- What happened (Layer 4: Insight Computation)
- When it happened (Layer 3: Time and Structure)
- How to view it (Layer 5: Lens Layer)

But it cannot tell you:
- What it means in the arc of your life
- How this moment connects to who you are becoming
- What patterns persist across time

This gap exists because the system is **reflecting** but not **witnessing**.

Reflecting shows you what is there.
Witnessing holds what matters over time.

---

## Witnessing Behaviors

The Observer layer would exhibit these behaviors:

### Remembering What Mattered Before
- Notices when themes from months ago resurface
- Holds context from previous periods
- Connects current insights to past patterns

### Noticing Drift
- Detects when your attention shifts
- Observes when patterns change
- Names transitions without interpreting them

### Naming Recurring Tensions
- Identifies themes that persist across time windows
- Holds contradictions without resolving them
- Recognizes when the same tension appears in different forms

### Holding Themes Without Forcing Resolution
- Presents patterns without prescribing action
- Allows ambiguity to remain ambiguous
- Does not conclude, only observes

---

## Design and Logic Constraints

The Observer layer must adhere to these constraints:

### No Urgency
- Never creates a sense of "you need to act now"
- Does not prioritize recent over important
- Time moves slowly, not quickly

### No "You Should"
- Never prescribes behavior
- Never suggests optimization
- Never coaches or advises

### No Notification Feel
- Does not announce itself
- Does not interrupt
- Does not demand attention

### Invite, Not Interrupt
- Presents itself when you seek it
- Does not push itself forward
- Available but not insistent

### Slow by Default
- Encourages contemplation, not action
- Supports reflection, not reaction
- Time moves at the pace of thought

---

## Refusals and Boundaries

The Observer layer **refuses** to:

### Infer Meaning
- Does not conclude what patterns "mean"
- Does not interpret your intentions
- Does not assume causality

### Conclude
- Does not resolve tensions
- Does not provide answers
- Does not close loops

### Prescribe
- Does not suggest actions
- Does not recommend changes
- Does not optimize

### Gamify
- Does not track streaks
- Does not reward consistency
- Does not measure progress

### Interrupt
- Does not notify
- Does not alert
- Does not demand

---

## Success Criteria

The Observer layer succeeds when:

### Feels Like Accompaniment Over Time
- You feel witnessed, not analyzed
- You feel held, not measured
- You feel accompanied, not coached

### Quiet Center of Gravity
- It is present but not loud
- It provides stability without demanding attention
- It is the still point around which insights orbit

### Discovered, Not Served
- You find it when you seek it
- It reveals itself through exploration
- It does not announce itself

### Continuity Without Closure
- It holds your story without concluding it
- It provides context without resolution
- It witnesses without interpreting

---

## Implementation Guidance (Without Code)

The Observer layer should:

### Sit Between Void and Signals
- It is not the Void (which is empty)
- It is not Signals (which are immediate)
- It is the continuity that connects them

### Be the Closure Step, Not Feature Expansion
- It is not about adding new insights
- It is about holding existing insights over time
- It provides narrative closure, not feature completeness

### Emerge from Existing Data
- It does not require new data models
- It works with what already exists
- It is a stance applied to existing insights

### Be Invisible Until Sought
- It does not appear in navigation
- It is not a menu item
- It reveals itself through use

---

## Relationship to Other Layers

The Observer layer:
- **Depends on** Layer 4 (Insight Computation) for patterns to observe
- **Depends on** Layer 5 (Lens Layer) for perspectives to hold over time
- **Depends on** Layer 6 (Sharing) for boundaries to respect
- **Provides** continuity that other layers cannot

It is the **final layer** because it requires all other layers to be stable before it can be built.

---

## When It Can Be Built

The Observer layer can be built when:
1. All layers below it are mechanically complete
2. The experiential articulation of lenses is complete
3. The sharing ritual is defined
4. The system feels stable, not in flux

It is **not** built by adding features.
It is built by adopting a witnessing stance.
